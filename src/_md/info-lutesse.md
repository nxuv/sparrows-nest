### *The year is 2405*

Almost twenty years after the brutal fall of the Avignon Empire, its once-shimmering capital city still stands as little more than a broken monument to fascist folly. The terror bombings may have stopped, and the foreign peacekeepers are mostly gone, but no economic redevelopment program can truly make up for what was lost.

Siblings, cousins, parents - everyone lost someone back then.
In the face of solitude and devastation, many of those who did banded together and escaped into the ruins - became misfits, or "Racailles". Back then, it was for survival: it was that, or waiting to get mulched by a rogue imperial mech plowing through your assigned refugee camp. Today, it's for belonging: to not die inside while making ends meet in a city that, by design, still feels like a prison to most.

### *A new and improved Nouvelle Lutesse - newer, sleeker and more angular than ever before.*

Back then, those "Racailles" skated because it was fast, agile and took the edge off. It's not like you could just drive around what remained of the boulevards: first, because you never knew when a fight could break out between army remnants and peacekeepers; second, because you sure as hell didn't own an unexploded car. Twenty years later, they do because Alliance architects have unwittingly turned the city into a veritable skater paradise - and nowhere else is more true than in the West Side, home to most of the city's best skating spots.

### *So clock out, get your board and come shred them.*

**Radio Free Lutesse** is a GMless, 1-to-4 players skate session RPG Inspired by [Bomb Rush Cyberfunk](https://www.gog.com/en/game/bomb_rush_cyberfunk), **Thrasher: Skate & Destroy** and **La Haine**. A game about skater **misfits** and a **city that doesn't want them anymore**. You are one of such misfits: a **Racaille** (often shortened to **"Rack"**), and you're out there in your **offtime**, hitting one of the **West Side's** many street skating spots. You may be doing so alone or with friends, and you know that if you make **too much ruckus** you'll find yourself running from a variety of **pissed off civil servants**: from **disgruntled foreign peacekeepers** to **private security in souped-up standup scooters**. Still, you shred - **because fuck'em**.

Featuring a **4-stat dicepool system** very loosely inspired by the **World of Darkness** games, **Radio Free Lutesse** is all about **agility, chaos and drama**: a single **skating session** plays something like a **narrative board game**, in which each player takes a turn attempting a trick off of the architectural features visible on your chosen spot's **postcard**. After every round, **passerbys** of different kinds will come snoop on your session - all while your **mistakes** and **failed tricks** slowly but steadily attract the attention of **nearby cops**. Whether you're **chased off the spot** or **call the session of your own volition**, that can be considered a **full session** of **Radio Free Lutesse**.

In case you got particularly attached to your **Racks** however, the game also offers a couple of mechanics for **continued "campaigns" through multiple skating sessions** - mainly with the Hangout and Board Shop systems, with which you may further the relationship between your characters and the city itself, give each other bonuses or debuffs, get really angry about posers waiting in line at the crab shop or replace your broken custom board with the one piece of cheap garbage you can actually afford.

In a manner inspired by [Hardcase](https://thursday-garreau.itch.io/hardcase), many of this game's systems are **optional** and **modular**: meaning you can very well play with just your **4 stats** and a **pre-generated board** if you're just here to skate - or give your Rack a specific **clothing style, graffiti crew, horrible dayjob** and **up to three, potentially contraddictory political fixations** if you want to truly get RPG with it.

### *You caught all that? Then tune in and turn up: it's Radio Free Lutesse.*
